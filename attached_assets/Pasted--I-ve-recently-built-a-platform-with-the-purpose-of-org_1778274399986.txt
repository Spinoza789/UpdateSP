Bottlenecks & failure points in Section 1
1. Backfilling existing orders When you add the new routing_type field, every existing order in the database has no value for it. You need to backfill them correctly — orders with directShippingRequested = true become direct, orders with a reshipperUsername become reshipper, everything else becomes unrouted. The risk is that some of those "everything else" orders are already shipped and fulfilled — they'd incorrectly show as needing a routing decision. The backfill logic needs to account for order status, not just the routing fields.
2. Two sources of truth during changeover The old fields (directShippingRequested, reshipperUsername) and the new routing_type field will coexist. Any part of the codebase that still updates the old fields without also updating routing_type will cause them to disagree. They'll silently go out of sync and you won't know until something looks wrong on the board or FS3.
3. Vendor shipping cost split breaks for cross-border orders Currently the vendor shipping cost is divided equally among all orders in a country leg. If 2 Ireland orders are in the Netherlands reshipper's batch, which rate do they pay — Ireland's or Netherlands'? The data model change doesn't resolve this. You need a deliberate decision: either cross-border orders pay the reshipper hub's rate, or they carry a custom cost set by the admin.
4. Reshipper capacity with no fallback If all reshippers for a country are at capacity, new orders pile up as unrouted silently. Without an alert, you won't know until you check the board manually. Needs an admin notification when orders can't be auto-assigned.
5. Priority conflicts If two reshippers in the same country have the same priority number, auto-assignment is undefined — it'll just pick one arbitrarily. Needs to enforce unique priority per country grouping.
6. Schema push risk Your startup runs drizzle-kit push --force directly against production. The new column needs a safe default value so existing rows don't break the app before the backfill runs.

Personal additions
Straightforward to handle — when you add a personal item, you choose which routing group it goes into (Direct, Netherlands Reshipper, UK Reshipper A, etc.). Once assigned it behaves like any other order in that batch: it contributes to the kit count, affects the tier, and appears on that group's output sheet. Bottlenecks and failure points in FS3
1. Missing prices breaking P&L If a product in the order has no entry in your "My Prices" list, the profit calculation silently uses zero — or worse, omits that product entirely. You could send a vendor order and only realise the P&L was wrong after. Needs a hard block: if any product in the batch has no wholesale price set, FS3 won't let you generate the sheet until it's added.
2. Including unconfirmed payments If an order with a pending or partial payment accidentally gets included in the vendor batch, you're shipping product you haven't been paid for. Needs a clear gate — unconfirmed payment orders are excluded by default, and including them manually requires a deliberate override with a reason logged.
3. Kit count changing after batch is built If a customer amends their order after you've already built the batch — changes quantities, adds a product — the tier calculation is now wrong. The vendor shipping split is wrong. Everything downstream is wrong. Orders that are amended after being included in a batch need to be flagged immediately with the impact shown.
4. Personal additions pushing a tier Adding your personal items to a batch increases the kit count, which might push the batch into a higher tier and change every other order's vendor shipping share. Same cascade as moving an order — needs the cost preview before confirming.
5. Multi-vendor GBs If a GB has products from more than one vendor, the output sheet needs to be split by vendor — you can't send one combined list to multiple vendors. Currently the vendor filter exists but the sheet generation doesn't enforce the split. Each vendor needs their own clean sheet from the same batch.
6. Wholesale price changes mid-GB If you update a price in "My Prices" after orders were placed, the P&L for every existing order in the GB changes retroactively. You might not notice. Needs a flag: "X prices have changed since this GB opened — review the impact before generating."
7. Currency inconsistency The wholesale rate table is in USD. Customer payments might be in GBP, EUR, or USD. If the P&L mixes currencies without converting, the numbers are meaningless. Every calculation needs to be anchored to one currency with conversions clearly shown.
8. No pre-submission verification Right now nothing stops you from downloading a sheet that's incomplete or wrong. A pre-flight check before generation would catch everything — missing prices, unconfirmed payments, missing addresses on direct orders, unresolved balance dues — before it becomes a mistake sent to the vendor.

	•	All balance dues are resolved
	•	No orders amended after batch was built (or amendments reviewed)
